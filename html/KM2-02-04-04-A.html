Tips for Parents

In Level D, students are transitioning to more advanced phonics concepts such as consonant digraphs, consonant blends, and silent -e. The level also returns to nouns, but now looks at common irregular plural nouns, an important concept for students to master. Adjectives are also introduced, and verbs are studied more in depth.
Give your child lots of opportunities to read aloud. Inspire your child to practice every day. Let your child re-read favorite books and poems. Re-reading helps kids read more quickly and accurately. Help your child dig deeper into story. Ask your child questions about the story you’ve just read. Say something like, “Why do you think Sam did that?” When your child is trying to sound out unfamiliar word, give him or her time to do so.
